Lists

There are two methods of displaying a list in html. first, you have the unordered list. the is a UL tag. Next, you have the ordered list. This is displayed using the ol tag. Unordered lists are for things that have no real order, maybe a shopping list. Whereas, the ordered list has an order. this could be a recipe. Items are displayed in the list with an LI.

Anchors and Links

To go from place to place, or more specifically, surf the web a web page must have an anchor tag and an href. The combination of those two will enable the webdeveloper to build the web. For example, the following link: www.google.com will take you to google. And this link : TOP OF PAGE will take you to the top of the page.

Text Emphasis

Sometimes, a developer wants to emphazise parts of the page. That would be for accessibility purposes. For example, to stress a particular word, like, "she screamed get out of the water now!!!!"", the devloper would wrap the text in an em tag. the em tag would give the text an italized formatting and it would tell screen readers, for example, to stress that word. or maybe a phrase has strong importance. for example, "With our dog, no matter how much he begs, we never feed him after 10pm".
